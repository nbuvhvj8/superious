# Strategic Roadmap for Placeholder Tabs

This document outlines three high-impact options for utilizing the **Chat**, **Cron Job**, and **Storages** tabs to enhance the value proposition of the outlier research engine.

---

## Option 1: The "Production Assistant" Workflow
**Focus:** Turning raw research into collaborative, iterative production.

### 1. Chat: AI Creative Partner
*   **Why:** Users often need to refine a script or brainstorm angles after seeing the initial research. A static script isn't enough; they need a partner to "tweak the hook" or "make it punchier."
*   **How:** Implement a standard chat interface connected to the script's context. Allow users to highlight script sections and ask the AI to "rewrite this for a Gen-Z audience" or "expand on the Netflix section."

### 2. Cron Job: Competitive Intelligence
*   **Why:** Video topics go stale fast. Creators need to know *when* a new development occurs on a topic they've already researched.
*   **How:** Allow users to schedule "Recapture" jobs. If a topic is set to weekly, the system re-scans the web and notifies the user via the Notification Modal if a major new source or conflicting data is found.

### 3. Storages: Asset & B-Roll Management
*   **Why:** Research is just one part of the pipeline. Creators need to manage the visual assets (screenshots, generated B-Roll prompts) in one place.
*   **How:** A file-explorer style view that organizes all captured screenshots, exported PDFs, and generated script versions by job ID or collection.

---

## Option 2: The "Enterprise Research" Suite
**Focus:** Scaling content production for teams and agencies.

### 1. Chat: Team Collaboration & Review
*   **Why:** In agencies, research needs internal feedback before it reaches the client.
*   **How:** Use the chat for team comments. Enable `@mentions` to notify team members about specific research findings or script drafts.

### 2. Cron Job: Automated Content Engine
*   **Why:** Many creators post on a fixed schedule (e.g., "Tech News Tuesday").
*   **How:** A dashboard to schedule entire jobs in advance. "Every Monday at 9 AM, research [Current Trending Tech Topic] and have a script ready by noon."

### 3. Storages: Multi-Platform Export Sync
*   **Why:** Data needs to live where the production happens (Google Drive, Dropbox, Notion).
*   **How:** A configuration panel for external cloud storages. Users can map collections to specific folders in Google Drive or Notion databases.

---

## Option 3: The "Deep Intelligence" Platform
**Focus:** Advanced data analysis and long-term knowledge storage.

### 1. Chat: Data Query Interface
*   **Why:** When dealing with 50+ sources, finding a specific quote or stat is hard.
*   **How:** A "Chat with your Sources" feature. Instead of creative writing, it focuses on retrieval: "What was the exact revenue for Netflix in 2011 mentioned in the sources?"

### 2. Cron Job: Archive Snapshots
*   **Why:** Web pages disappear. Creators need a "wayback machine" for their specific research niche.
*   **How:** Schedule recurring snapshots of specific URL lists to ensure the evidence for a script remains accessible even if the original site goes down.

### 3. Storages: Semantic Knowledge Base
*   **Why:** Research shouldn't be siloed by "Job." Insights from one research job might be relevant to another.
*   **How:** A "Knowledge Graph" view where sources are tagged semantically. If two different jobs both use a source about "Streaming Wars," they are linked here for easy cross-referencing.
